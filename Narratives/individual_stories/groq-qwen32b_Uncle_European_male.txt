**Title: Luca and the Clockmaker’s Secret**  

Once, in a quaint village nestled between the Alps, lived young Luca, whose family had crafted clocks for generations. His favorite was the towering grandfather clock in his shop, its gears whispering tales of time. Luca loved dismantling it, eager to master its secrets.  

One day, impatient to “fix” it faster, he hurled a gear too hard, shattering it. Panicked, he called his grandfather, the village’s wisest clockmaker.  

Grandfather sighed, “Time isn’t a race, *bambino*. Each gear has its pace. Rush, and you break what cannot be mended.” He handed Luca a tiny gear. “Let’s find its match—not today, but *tomorrow*, with steady hands.”  

The next day, they searched slowly, restoring the clock with care. As it ticked to life, Luca understood: *Patience turns chaos into harmony.*  

Years later, Luca became a master clockmaker, teaching his own children, “Tradition isn’t just in timepieces—it’s in how we honor them.”  

**Lesson:** True mastery comes not from haste, but from respecting the rhythm of learning.
